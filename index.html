Industrial Activities

Industrial processes such as manufacturing, mining, and waste disposal can release a wide range of pollutants into the soil.Chemicals like heavy metals (e.g., lead, mercury, cadmium), solvents, acids, and toxic organic compounds may contaminate soil through spills, leaks, improper disposal practices, and industrial runoff.

Agricultural Practices

Agricultural activities are a significant source of soil pollution, mainly due to the use of fertilizers, pesticides, and herbicides.Excessive use or improper application of these chemicals can lead to their accumulation in the soil, causing soil degradation, loss of biodiversity, and contamination of groundwater.
